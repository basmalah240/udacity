Toucan

Toucans regulate their body temperature by adjusting blood flow to their massive, colorful beaks. Their beaks are also surprisingly light despite their size, made mostly of keratin!

  • Scientific Name: Ramphastos toco.
  • Average Length: 55–65 cm.
  • Average Lifespan: 15–20 years.
  • Habitat: Tropical rainforests, savannas, and woodland areas of Central and South America.

The toucan is a tropical bird known for its large, colorful beak, which helps with temperature regulation and feeding. Found in Central and South American rainforests, it primarily eats fruits, but also consumes insects, eggs, and small reptiles. Despite its bulky beak, the toucan is lightweight and agile, using its strong legs and short wings to navigate trees. It is a social bird, often living in small flocks, and communicates with croaking calls.
